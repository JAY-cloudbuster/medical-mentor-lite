Core Pathology Term

Atrial Fibrillation

description Definition

A supraventricular tachyarrhythmia characterized by rapid, uncoordinated atrial activation with consequent deterioration of atrial mechanical function. On ECG, it is defined by the replacement of consistent P waves by rapid oscillations or fibrillatory waves.

analytics Pathophysiology

Often results from re-entrant wavelets within the pulmonary veins or left atrium. Remodeling of the atrial substrate over time leads to permanent conduction changes.

clinical_notes Clinical Relevance

High risk for thromboembolic stroke. Management involves rhythm or rate control and anticoagulation based on CHADS2-VASc scores.
